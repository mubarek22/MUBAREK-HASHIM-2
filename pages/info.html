What is a Permanent Resident Card?
A permanent resident card is a immigrant visa to the United States of America also popularly known as a Green Card due to its green color. It allows foreign nationals to legally live, study and in the US and to enter and leave the United States freely. Permanent Resident card holders receive healthcare, education, and other benefits, and they can sponsor their relatives for permanent resident Cards too. Permanent residents are people who have been granted the right to live and work in the United States of America on a permanent basis. A Permanent Resident card is the proof of that status. What is the Green Card Lottery program?
The Green Card Lottery program is the popular name for the Diversity Visa Immigrant Lottery Program that is administered by the United States Department of State. This immigration program makes available permanent residence visas to foreign nationals that meet certain requirements. Winners of the DV lottery are chosen randomly in a computer drawing that is conducted by the United States Department of State.
Who is allowed to participate in the DV2025 Lottery?
The applicant must be a native of a country whose natives qualify and must meet either the education or training requirement of the program. To check if you qualify, go to the green card lottery requirements page.
Can I include my spouse and children?
Yes, applicants can include their spouse and children on their DV2025 lottery application. if you are selected in the DV2025 lottery and obtain a Permanent Resident Card, your spouse and unmarried children under the age of 21 may also submit documentation to receive a Permanent Resident Cards as well.
Is there a minimum age to apply?
No, there is no minimum age to apply for the DV2025 immigration lottery program. The requirement of a high school education or work experience for each principal applicant at the time of application will however effectively disqualify most applicants who are under 18 years of age.
